In each chart the two typologies are presented side by side, for both men and women over 60. The lightly shaded background represents the distribution for the population as a whole.

\mini{\parbox[c]
{13.5cm}{

\maxi 
The prevalence of different household living arrangements varies across countries and across different population groups. This factsheet focuses on people over 60 years of age and compares men and women in each country. 
\textcolor{white}{\cite{gh2017}}
\vspace{1ex}

Using census microdata collected in the IPUMS-International database \citep{impus2017} we summarise the types of households in which the elderly live using  two typologies:
 \begin{itemize}
 \item the household composition based on family nuclei, and
 \item the intergenerational structure of the household. \end{itemize}  

The proportions displayed are of \emph{individuals} living in each type of household (not proportions of households of each type) and show important differences between genders, as well broader patterns that can be observed regionally and globally. The living arrangements of elderly people can also be contrasted to the average of the population as a whole, which is displayed as the background of each barchart.
